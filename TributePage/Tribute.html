Tribute to Persons with Disabilities (PWDs) in the Philippines

Persons with Disabilities (PWDs) in the Philippines continue to prove that true strength lies in perseverance and purpose. Through the years, the nation has taken important steps to recognize their rights, promote inclusion, and celebrate their achievements.
Timeline of Progress

The following list is a time line of Onassis Life

  • 1981 – International Year of Disabled Persons - The Philippines joined the global movement to promote awareness and equal opportunities for PWDs.
  • 1992 – Republic Act No. 7277 (Magna Carta for Disabled Persons) - This landmark law guaranteed PWDs access to education, employment, and social services.
  • 2000s – Accessibility and Advocacy - Government offices and local units began improving facilities, launching livelihood programs, and celebrating National Disability Prevention and Rehabilitation (NDPR) Week every July.
  • 2016 – Republic Act No. 10754 - Expanded benefits and privileges for PWDs, including discounts and tax incentives.
  • Today - PWD advocates, athletes, and professionals continue to break barriers and inspire inclusion nationwide.
